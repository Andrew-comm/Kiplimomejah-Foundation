Flood Effects in Kenya

Flooding in Kenya has severe impacts:

  • Loss of Lives: People are swept away by rapidly rising waters.
  • Displacement: Thousands flee to temporary shelters or makeshift camps.
  • Destruction of Homes: Houses are destroyed, leaving families homeless.
  • Infrastructure Damage: Roads, bridges, and buildings are washed away, disrupting transport and communication.
  • Contamination of Water Sources: Floods spread waterborne diseases.
  • Livelihood Loss: Agriculture suffers, leading to food shortages.
  • Environmental Degradation: Soil erosion, deforestation, and loss of biodiversity.

Aftermath: Communities face immense suffering and long-term challenges.
